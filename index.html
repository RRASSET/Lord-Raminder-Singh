ABOUT

LORD RAMI RANGER FRSA

Lord Rami Ranger is an award-winning entrepreneur, philanthropist, and advocate for diversity. His life’s work has been dedicated to fostering political and social change, supporting charitable causes, and strengthening ties between communities.

CHARITIES & PHILANTHROPY

Supporting Student Facilities:
His £200,000 donation to the University of West London funded a new pavilion for students.

Thames Hospice:
Lord Rami donated £100,000 to support compassionate end-of-life care.

Gandhi Memorial Foundation Trust:
He donated £100,000 towards the Gandhi statue in Parliament Square.

Help for Heroes:
Lord Rami helped raise £25,000 to support wounded veterans.

Graduate Entrepreneurship Centre:
He donated £250,000 to London South Bank University for aspiring entrepreneurs.

Pakistan, India & UK Friendship Forum:
Founded after 7/7 to promote dialogue, peace and cooperation.

Cancer Research UK:
He donated £50,000 to support research in the fight against cancer.

Hindu Forum of Britain:
As a founder member, he helped unite Hindu organisations across the UK.

Indian Gymkhana:
He contributed £125,000 to build accommodation for athletes.

AIDS and Cancer Research:

Combat Stress:
He donated £25,000 to support mental health services for veterans.

The Prince's Trust:
He donated £150,000 to help young people build skills and confidence.
