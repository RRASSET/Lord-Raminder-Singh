EARLY LIFE

INSPIRATIONAL BEGINNINGS

Rami Ranger’s journey began in Patiala, India, where he was born two months after the assassination of his father, Shaheed Nanak Singh, who gave his life during the Partition of India while saving 600 students trapped in communal riots. Raised by his mother, a schoolteacher, in a refugee camp, Rami learned the values of hard work, empathy, and integrity from an early age.

HONOURING A LEGACY OF COURAGE

VALUES THAT ENDURED

Rami Ranger’s father was a man of courage and integrity. During the Partition of India in 1947, he stood by his values and sacrificed his life in the face of violence. His bravery left a lasting imprint on his family.

Though Rami Ranger was very young when he lost his father, the values he instilled — courage, honesty, and doing what is right — became a guiding force throughout his life.

Learn More

After relocating to the United Kingdom, Ranger began his journey by taking on a series of hands-on roles, demonstrating both resilience and determination. He initially worked as a chef at KFC in Norbury, South London, where his dedication quickly earned him a promotion to district manager. Building on this momentum, he went on to run a Post Office branch and a newsagent’s shop, later gaining further retail experience with Currys. These formative roles laid the foundation for his strong work ethic and entrepreneurial mindset.

BUSINESS

BUILDING EMPIRE

From these humble beginnings, Rami’s determination led him to the UK, where he established Sun Mark Ltd and Sea, Air & Land Forwarding Ltd—two of Britain’s fastest-growing companies. Sun Mark Ltd has achieved an unprecedented feat: five consecutive Queen’s Awards for Enterprise in International Trade, a testament to Rami’s business acumen and leadership.
